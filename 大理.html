Dali(大理), located in the southwestern province of Yunnan, China, is a captivating destination known for its enchanting landscapes, rich history, and diverse culture.

Surrounded by the picturesque Cangshan Mountain and facing the serene Erhai Lake, Dali boasts breathtaking natural beauty. The city's old town, with its well-preserved traditional Bai architecture and cobblestone streets, exudes a charming and nostalgic atmosphere.

Dali has a long and fascinating history that dates back to ancient times, and it was an important trading post along the ancient Tea Horse Road. Visitors can explore historical relics such as the Three Pagodas, which are iconic symbols of the region's cultural heritage.

The Bai ethnic minority is the dominant group in Dali, and their unique customs and traditions add to the city's cultural appeal. Don't miss the chance to experience Bai folk performances and savor their delectable cuisine, known for its use of fresh ingredients and bold flavors.

For nature enthusiasts, Dali offers a variety of outdoor activities, including hiking in the Cangshan Mountains, cycling around Erhai Lake, and exploring the nearby ancient villages. With its blend of history, culture, and stunning landscapes, Dali is a gem in Yunnan that promises an unforgettable travel experience.
