Crossing-the-bridge noodles have over a century of history and are listed as an intangible cultural heritage of Kunming city in 2008 to promote Yunnan food culture.

Steam Pot Chicken: The special steamer with a vent in the center is used. Place the chicken pieces inside the steamer, and steam them over boiling water. Steam enters the steamer through the vent in the center and cooks the chicken. The soup is formed by condensation of the steam, making it purer and free from impurities.

In Yunnan dialect, potatoes are called "洋芋" (yáng yù), which literally means "foreigner's taro." Although fried potatoes are a popular dish worldwide, Yunnan's version of fried potatoes has a unique seasoning. Yunnan locals use a variety of spices, such as cilantro, fish mint, chili, garlic, and more.

Yunnan is home to a wide variety of wild mushrooms. Although some of them are poisonous, the majority are edible. Due to the inability to cultivate them artificially, the prices of these mushrooms can often be exorbitant. However, their exceptionally high nutritional value and unique flavors make Yunnan locals passionate about consuming these mushrooms.
